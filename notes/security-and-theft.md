# Security and theft checklist

The stolen EV6 experience means security must be treated as a core buying criterion, not an afterthought.

## What to check for every candidate

### Vehicle-level security

- Keyless-entry relay attack mitigation.
- Whether keyless entry can be disabled easily.
- Quality of alarm, immobiliser and deadlocking.
- Whether the car has factory tracking, stolen-vehicle assistance or remote immobilisation.
- Whether the mobile app exposes risky functionality or weak account recovery paths.
- Whether digital key / phone-as-key is available, reliable and secure.
- Whether OTA updates have a credible security track record.

### Practical ownership setup

- Compatibility with a Thatcham-approved tracker if required by insurance.
- Ghost/aftermarket immobiliser compatibility and warranty implications.
- Faraday pouch need for keys.
- Physical anti-theft options: steering lock, driveway bollard, wheel clamp, parking camera/security lighting.
- Insurance premium/loadings versus the EV6 GT benchmark.
- Whether the model is appearing in theft reports or insurer concern lists.

### Scoring guidance

Use the `security` score in `docs/data/vehicles.json`.

- `9-10`: strong factory security plus credible tracking/immobilisation options and no obvious theft pattern.
- `7-8`: good baseline security, but some unknowns or aftermarket dependency.
- `5-6`: acceptable only if insurance and aftermarket mitigation are clear.
- `0-4`: major concern; should probably be rejected unless there is a compelling reason.
- `null`: insufficient evidence; keep as unknown rather than guessing.

## Red flags

- Keyless entry cannot be disabled.
- Insurance requires expensive tracker/immobiliser but dealer cannot confirm compatibility.
- App/digital key reviews mention lockouts, false unlocks, weak account security or repeated pairing issues.
- Manufacturer or dealer gives vague answers about stolen-vehicle support.
- Model is materially more expensive or difficult to insure because of theft risk.

## Minimum recommendation before ordering

Before signing a lease, collect:

1. Insurance quote for the exact trim.
2. Written confirmation of tracker/immobiliser requirements.
3. Confirmation that keyless entry can be disabled or mitigated.
4. Confirmation of factory tracking/stolen vehicle assistance, if any.
5. Dealer answer on whether aftermarket security affects warranty.
